Flexbox Interactive Demo

Note: the demo is at the end of this page of notes.

Flexbox can take a little while to get the hang of sometimes.

The principles of Flexbox are simple. Flexbox is used to align items in a container. The container has properties and the individual items can have properties too.

Opting-in

First there is opting-in to flex. For your container, you add the following code:

display: flex

Sizing

To make your flex container expand to fill the available space you can do:

flex: 1

Or you can give it a width and height like so:

width: 300;

height: 300;

flex-direction

The next thing to consider is flex-direction. Do you want your items to be laid out in a column:

flex-direction: column;

or in a row:

flex-direction: row;

flex-wrap

Then there is flex-wrap, which determines if your items will be wrapped within the container:

flex-wrap: wrap;

or not:

flex-wrap: nowrap;

justify-content

Then there is justify-content, which determines how items will be laid out in the same direction as the row or column.

align-content

Then there is align-content, which determines how items will be laid out perpendicular to the row or column.

align-items

Then there is align-items, which determines how items will be laid out perpendicular to the row or column but within the row or column.

Item Properties

There are also properties of the flex items: align-self, flex-basis, flex-shrink, and flex-grow. I will not get into them here.
